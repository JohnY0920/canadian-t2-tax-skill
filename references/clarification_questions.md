# Clarification Questions Template

After reviewing the source documents, generate a tailored list of questions from the categories below. Only ask questions that are relevant based on what you found in the documents. Present them as a numbered checklist.

---

## Category 1: Company Information

Ask these if not found in the uploaded documents:

- What is the full legal name of the corporation?
- What is the Business Number (BN) with CRA? (Format: 9 digits + RC0001)
- What is the corporation's registered address?
- What is the fiscal year end date? (Most CCPCs use Dec 31)
- Is this the corporation's first T2 filing, or has it filed before?
- Is the corporation a Canadian Controlled Private Corporation (CCPC)?

## Category 2: Unidentified Deposits

For each deposit that cannot be matched to an invoice or known source:

- "[Date]: $[amount] deposit from [description]. Is this business income? If so, who was the client and what service was provided?"
- For deposits from "MB-DEP Hamilton ON": "Is this a CRA refund (HST or income tax)? These are typically government direct deposits and should NOT be counted as business income."

## Category 3: Transfers to Personal Accounts

For any transfer from the business account to a personal account:

- "$[amount] transferred to [account] on [date]. Is this:
  (a) Repayment of shareholder loan (not taxable, not deductible)
  (b) Salary/wages (deductible to company, taxable to you -- requires T4)
  (c) Dividend payment (not deductible, taxable to you -- requires T5)
  (d) Personal expense reimbursement"

This distinction matters enormously for tax treatment. Shareholder loan repayments are the most common for small CCPCs and have zero tax impact.

## Category 4: Mixed-Use Assets

For any asset that might have personal use:

- "The [asset name] purchased for $[amount] -- what percentage is used for business purposes? CRA requires documentation (usage log) if audited."
- "Is the company vehicle used for any personal trips? If yes, a taxable standby charge benefit must be included on your personal T1 return."

## Category 5: Missing Documentation

- "There are [N] deposits totalling $[total] without matching invoices. CRA requires invoices for all business income. Do you have invoices for these, or should I prepare template invoices that you can complete with client details?"
- "I don't see a prior-year financial statement. Do you have one? The opening balances (cash, retained earnings, shareholder loan) are needed to prepare the current year balance sheet."

## Category 6: Expense Clarification

For ambiguous expenses:

- "[Merchant name] charge of $[amount] on [date] -- is this a business expense? If so, what category?"
- "Interac e-transfer sent to [description] for $[amount] on [date] -- who was the recipient and what was this for? If it's a payment to a contractor/supplier, it's deductible (and may require a T4A if over $500 to one person in a year)."

## Category 7: Tax Elections & Special Situations

- "Did you pay dividends to any shareholders this year?"
- "Do you have any capital gains or losses to report (e.g., sale of investments, property, or equipment)?"
- "Is the consulting income from a personal services business? (i.e., would you be considered an employee of the client if not for the corporation?)"
- "Did you make any installment payments to CRA during the year?"
- "Do you have any investment income (interest, dividends received)?"
- "Are there any related-party transactions to report?"

## Category 8: HST-Specific

- "Is the corporation registered for HST/GST? (Check prior HST returns or CRA correspondence)"
- "For deposits where the amount doesn't match a standard invoiced amount -- did these include HST, or were they HST-exclusive?"
- "Was HST charged on the accounting fee payment? (Depends on whether the accountant is HST-registered)"

---

## How to Present Questions

Group questions by urgency:

**Must Answer Before Filing:**
- Business Number
- Unidentified large deposits
- Personal vs. business transfer classification

**Should Answer for Accuracy:**
- Mixed-use asset percentages
- Missing invoice client details

**Good to Clarify (Won't Block Filing):**
- Minor expense categorization
- HST on small amounts

Format as a numbered list with checkboxes the user can work through.
